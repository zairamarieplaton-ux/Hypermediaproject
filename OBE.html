Lesson 1 · Definition

What is Outcomes-Based Education?

OBE is a philosophy that organizes everything in the educational system around what students should successfully demonstrate at the end of their learning experiences.

▶ Watch: Overview of Outcomes-Based Education (YouTube)

Spady's 3 Premises (1994)
🌱 All students can learn and succeed, but not at the same time or in the same way.
✨ Successful learning promotes even more successful learning.
🏫 Schools and teachers control the conditions that determine if students are successful.
4 Questions Every Teacher Must Ask
What do we want students to learn?
Why do we want them to learn these things?
How can we best help students learn?
How will we know when they have learned?
Lesson 1 · Core Concepts

The 4 Essential Principles of OBE

These four principles guide every decision in planning, teaching, and assessing under an OBE framework.

1
Principle 1
Clarity of Focus
Teachers and students must have a clear picture of the knowledge, skills, and values to be achieved. The target must be visible before the journey begins.
2
Principle 2
Designing Backwards
Start with the end goal. Define outcomes first, then design the curriculum, instruction, and assessment to work backward from that desired result.
3
Principle 3
High Expectations
Challenging standards motivate better learning. Linked to Thorndike's Law of Effect: success reinforces learning, builds confidence, and encourages students to do more.
4
Principle 4
Expanded Opportunities
Every student can excel when given equal, expanded opportunities. Multiple intelligences remind us: every child has genius waiting to be nurtured.

5 Tips for OBE Teaching
📋
Prepare students adequatelyKnow your learning outcomes first. Review prerequisite knowledge before each lesson begins.
🌈
Create a positive learning environmentRespect diversity. The classroom should be a safe space for all kinds of learners.
🧩
Use appropriate strategiesMatch your methods to content, student characteristics, and resources available. OBE is learner-centered but not method-rigid.
🔬
Provide opportunities to apply learningApplication is encouraged over mere accumulation. Let students explore, correct errors, and adjust thinking.
🔚
Bring each lesson to personal closureHelp students become aware of what they have learned and reflect on its meaning.
Lesson 1 · Assessment

Assessment of Learning Outcomes

In OBE, assessment is not an afterthought — it is an embedded part of the system guided by the same four principles as instruction.

8 Principles
Valid
Reliable
Fair
Relevant
Informative
Supportive
Individual
Comprehensive
1
Valid — Assessment tools should measure exactly what they intend to test.
2
Reliable — Results should be consistent across different contexts or times.
3
Fair — Cultural background and other factors must not unfairly influence the assessment.
4
Relevant — Should reflect the knowledge and skills that actually matter to students' lives.
5
Informative — Must tell both teachers and students how students are progressing.
6
Supportive — Should support every student's opportunity to learn important things.
7
Individual — Allow individuality and uniqueness to be demonstrated in responses.
8
Comprehensive — Must cover a wide range of learning outcomes, not just recall.
Lesson 1 · Student Perspective

The Learner's Responsibility

In OBE, nobody can learn for the learner. Students own their learning journey — teachers only facilitate and guide. Tap each question to reflect.

1. What do I have to learn?
Check your course syllabus and the stated Desired Learning Outcomes (DLOs) — these define your targets for the semester.
›
2. Why do I have to learn it?
Connect each topic to real-world applications and your future teaching practice. Purpose drives motivation.
›
3. How will I know that I am learning?
Reflect regularly. Can you explain the concept in your own words? Can you apply it in a new situation? These are signs of real learning.
›
4. Will I have any say in what I learn?
OBE encourages some degree of student agency — through choice of outputs, project topics, or areas of focus within the given outcomes.
›
5. How will I be assessed?
Assessment should be aligned to your learning outcomes. Knowing this upfront allows you to prepare meaningfully, not just memorize.
›
Key Takeaway

OBE makes students aware of what they are doing when they are learning. This metacognitive awareness — knowing how to learn, not just what to learn — is one of its greatest benefits for future teachers like you.
